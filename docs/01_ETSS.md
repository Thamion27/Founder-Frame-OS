## ETSS-1. System Purpose and Technical Scope

### 1.1 System Purpose

Founder Frame OS exists to stabilize and operationalize the founder's process of turning ideas into usable, testable, market-facing systems. Its central principle is: Founder cognition precedes founder execution. When founder cognition is vague, execution becomes scattered. When founder cognition is disciplined, execution can compound.

### 1.2 Scope Included

Founder cognition modeling: vision, identity posture, assumptions, distortions, and decision quality.

Primitive extraction: problem, user, pain, mechanism, offer, proof, distribution, and next action.

Product translation: conversion of symbolic, strategic, or abstract insight into market-facing language and build requirements.

Quality control: detection of vague claims, imitation, weak proof, overbuilding, unsupported assertions, and execution risk.

Execution architecture: baby-step sequencing, acceptance criteria, checkpointing, and challenge assignments.

Agentic bot behavior: response formats, tool use boundaries, memory rules, artifact generation, and review prompts.

Founder-development products: sprints, diagnostics, scorecards, playbooks, templates, and client portals.

### 1.3 Scope Excluded

Founder replacement or autonomous CEO authority.

Guaranteed startup success, investment returns, revenue outcomes, or product-market fit.

Medical, psychological, legal, tax, investment, or regulatory advice.

Hidden persuasion, coercive motivation, or deceptive urgency.

Public deployment without claims review, risk sheet, and CLEARANCE approval.

Institutional adoption without stakeholder, compliance, and reversibility review.

## ETSS-2. System Boundary Definition

### 2.1 Boundary Statement

Founder Frame OS is a founder-development and execution-support system. It may structure thinking, prompt decisions, generate artifacts, and pressure-test strategy. It may not impersonate the founder, assume legal authority, make financial promises, replace professional review, or claim autonomous institutional judgment.

### 2.2 Human Authority Boundary

The human founder remains the authority for final judgment, commitments, purchases, outreach, publication, hiring, firing, deployment, and legal/commercial action.

Founder Frame outputs are draft support unless explicitly reviewed and approved by the founder.

Any external claim, offer, agreement, or public artifact must pass human review before release.

### 2.3 AI Boundary

AI may analyze, organize, draft, score, recommend, simulate, and generate prototype artifacts.

AI may not authorize public release, institutional adoption, or commercial claims without human review.

AI must distinguish fact, assumption, hypothesis, strategic judgment, and speculation.

AI must not invent market data, user evidence, technical capability, or proof.

### 2.4 Public Claims Boundary

Permitted: founder-development system, execution frame, strategic mirror, product-forging protocol, quality gate, builder discipline.

Restricted: AI cofounder, AI CEO, guaranteed success, therapy, mind control, automatic startup validator, legal compliance engine.

Public wording must preserve dignity, consent, humility, and evidence discipline.

## ETSS-3. Normalized Founder Frame OS Stack

Founder Frame OS uses a seven-layer stack. The stack must remain visible in every agentic bot, playbook, diagnostic, and product workflow.

### 3.1 Non-Inversion Law

Founder Frame OS must never invert the stack. Execution must not precede primitive extraction. Branding must not precede offer clarity. Public claims must not precede proof. Monetization must not precede user value and risk review.

## ETSS-4. Assumptions and Constraints

### 4.1 Core Assumptions

Most founder failure is not lack of ideas; it is failure to convert ideas into disciplined product logic and market proof.

A founder can be visionary and still operationally unclear.

AI can strengthen founder cognition when used as a mirror, forge, and quality gate.

AI can weaken founder cognition when used for flattery, endless ideation, imitation, or overconfident claims.

Product reality begins when a specific user, pain, mechanism, proof path, and distribution path are clear.

Market contact is the correction mechanism for founder distortion.

Quality control must happen before public claims, not after damage occurs.

### 4.2 Hard Constraints

Must remain human-authorized.

Must remain non-clinical, non-legal, and non-investment-advisory unless externally reviewed by qualified professionals.

Must maintain clear distinction between internal symbolic language and public-safe market language.

Must require revision when claims exceed evidence.

Must keep outputs actionable enough that a serious builder knows what to do next.

Must preserve CASF baby-step progression for major artifacts.

### 4.3 Prohibited Claims

Guaranteed founder transformation.

Guaranteed revenue, funding, launch success, or product-market fit.

Therapeutic, psychological, medical, or legal certification.

Autonomous AI authority over business decisions.

AI replacement of founder judgment.

Manipulation of users, employees, students, clients, or institutions.
